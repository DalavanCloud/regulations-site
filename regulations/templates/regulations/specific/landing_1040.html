The Arbitration Agreements Rule prohibits providers of covered consumer financial products or services from using pre-dispute arbitration agreements to block class actions and requires them to submit certain arbitration-related documents to the Bureau.

{% endblock %} {% block reg_main_content %}

Covered consumer financial products or services include:

  • Extending consumer credit
  • Acquiring or selling consumer credit
  • Servicing an extension of consumer credit
  • Participating in consumer credit decisions
  • Referring or selecting consumer creditors
  • Extending or brokering automobile leases
  • Providing debt management services
  • Providing debt settlement services
  • Providing credit repair services
  • Providing deposit or checking accounts
  • Providing remittance accounts and transfers
  • Providing payroll card accounts
  • Providing government benefit accounts
  • Providing consumer credit report information
  • Transmitting or exchanging funds
  • Providing certain payment processing services
  • Providing check cashing, collection, or guaranty services
  • Collecting consumer debt

The regulation covers topics such as:

  • Pre-dispute arbitration agreements
  • Class actions
  • Arbitration-related filings
